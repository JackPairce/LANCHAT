Create a New Google Sheet and Share

  1. Go to the Google Cloud Console.
  2. Create a new project or select an existing project.
  3. Enable the "Google Sheets API" for your project:
    • Click on the menu icon (â˜°) in the upper-left corner.
    • Navigate to "APIs & Services" -> "Library".
    • Search for "Google Sheets API" and select it.
    • Click on the "Enable" button.
  4. Create service account credentials:
    • In the left sidebar, go to "APIs & Services" -> "Credentials".
    • Click on the "Create credentials" button and select "Service account".
    • Fill in the necessary information (e.g., service account name, description).
    • Click on the "Create" button.
    • On the "Service account permissions" page, assign the required permissions (e.g., "Editor" access to the Google Sheet).
    • Click on the "Continue" button.
    • On the "Create key" page, select "JSON" as the key type.
    • Click on the "Create" button to download the JSON file containing the credentials.
  5. Obtain the spreadsheetId:
    • Create a new Google Sheet in your Google Drive.
    • Open the sheet and extract the spreadsheetId from the URL. It is the alphanumeric string between /d/ and /edit in the URL.
  6. Share the Google Sheet with the client_email:
    • Open the Google Sheet.
    • Click on the "Share" button in the top-right corner.
    • Enter the client_email (e.g., "account-verification*******************gserviceaccount.com") in the "People" field.
    • Assign the appropriate access level (e.g., "Editor", "Viewer") to the client email.
    • Click on the "Send" button.
